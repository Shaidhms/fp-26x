This is not guesswork. This is how school fees actually grow in private schools across Chennai. If anything, this is a conservative estimate.

The Real Danger

What happens if we don't act

🔴 With just my salary, the math doesn't work

Today, my monthly expenses are ₹69,860. My salary is ₹70,000-78,000. That leaves barely ₹8,900 per month for groceries, emergencies, and everything else. Nothing left to save for school fees.


Now add school fees that grow from ₹9,000/month to ₹46,000/month over the next 15 years. By 2030, we'll be spending more than I earn. The only option would be to take loans to pay school fees, which means more EMIs, more stress, and a debt cycle that's hard to escape.

🟢 Together (my salary + your ₹35K), we breathe easy

With both our salaries, the monthly pool is ₹1.13 lakh. After fixed expenses, we have ₹43,910 left. I'll manage groceries (under ₹20K), and the remaining ₹23,000+ goes straight into a school fund for Shaina and Shayan every month.


In just 2 months of saving together, we'd have enough for a full school term. No loans needed. No stress. Just comfortable, planned spending. And as my income grows through a better job and freelancing, things only get easier.

3

Clear credit card debt

We're paying ₹59,000+ in CC debt. At 3.5% monthly interest, that's money going to the bank instead of our children. Clear it, then redirect that money to savings.

4

Planning a better job + freelancing

I'm looking for a better-paying role and building freelance income on the side. Either one improving will add more breathing room. Both together would change our situation significantly.

Cut unnecessary costs

Already cancelled ChatGPT and Cosmofeed (₹2,650/month saved). Continuing to review what else can be reduced without hurting earning ability.
